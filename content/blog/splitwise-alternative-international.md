There's a Splitwise feedback post from 2014 about a trip to Vietnam. Four people, three currencies, and at the end, in the user's own words, "it all turned bad." The interface was built for one currency. Settling across three was an unholy mess. That post is now eleven years old. The thread it sits in has more than twenty variants. Splitwise has not fixed any of them.

If you've ever tried to use Splitwise on an international trip, you already know why I'm writing this. The rates are wrong. The settlement rail stops at the US border. The free tier got crippled in 2024. And if you're looking for a Splitwise alternative international travelers can actually rely on, you'll quickly find that most of the listicles ranking for that search miss the only thing that matters: how do you actually settle the money at the end of the trip?

I'm the founder of Splito, and yes, I'll mention it once near the end. Most of this post is about the broader category and which app you should pick for which situation. If you're a US-based group traveling domestically with Venmo, the answer is mostly "stay with Splitwise Pro and accept the rate margin." If you ever travel with anyone who lives outside the US, the answer is different.

## How Splitwise breaks the moment you cross a border

Three things go wrong, in roughly this order.

First, the exchange rate Splitwise applies is interbank, not what you actually paid. Users have documented a 5% gap between Splitwise's auto-fetched rate and Google's. Andrey Rachev wrote exactly this on the feedback board: "I don't know how the pro app gets the conversion rate but it is different with 5% comparing to the Google conversion rate." On a two-week trip with $2,000 of group spend, a 5% gap is $100 silently misallocated. There is no override. You pay Pro to get this feature and then spend the trip arguing about whose card statement is the source of truth.

Second, the historical-rate problem is worse. If you upgrade to Pro mid-trip or settle weeks after the trip, Splitwise applies today's rate to old expenses. One user wrote on the feedback board that this caused "one of my friends to pay over $100 more than she should have" because the dollar moved against the Icelandic krona between the trip and the conversion. Money silently moved. Nobody noticed for weeks. By the time anyone did, asking for the difference felt insane, so she ate it.

Third, settlement. There is no payment rail. Splitwise integrates Venmo, which is US-only, and Tink, which is EU-only. Outside those geographies, the app shows you a balance and tells you to figure it out yourself. PayPal charges 5% plus an FX spread. Wire takes days. For everyone else, it's "settle in cash when you see them next," which for international friend groups means never.

There is also a request from 2019, with hundreds of votes, that Splitwise still has not built. The user wrote: "If I owe my friend 20GBP and she owes me 20USD, we should be able to convert that to a single currency." Cross-currency netting. The basic feature. Seven years later, still missing.

## The free tier in 2026 is barely a free tier

If the international gaps weren't enough, Splitwise tightened the screws on the free tier in late 2023. The current state: three expenses per day, a ten-second unskippable ad between each one, banner ads throughout the app, and the multi-currency feature paywalled at $4.99/month or $59.88/year.

Artem Russakovskii put it bluntly on X: "Users are rightfully pissed off at Splitwise for paywalling many of the previously free features without any notice and are leaving them in droves." That post is from late 2023 and the exodus has not stopped. Reddit threads in 2024 and 2025 are full of "what Splitwise alternative am I supposed to use now."

The math on Pro for an international group: $59.88 a year for currency conversion that's wrong by 5%, plus a settlement integration that doesn't work outside the US. You're paying $60 to be told an inaccurate number.

## What international travelers actually need

Strip the listicle framing and focus on what the trip actually requires.

Multi-currency entry without a paywall. You should not pay $60 a year to type in baht.

Locked exchange rates at the moment you spent the money. The rate when you tapped your card is the rate. Today's rate is irrelevant for an expense that happened three weeks ago.

Cross-currency netting. The Lauren R request from 2019. If two people owe each other in different currencies, the app should net it automatically.

Offline operation. Most international travel happens with patchy data. The app should keep working without it.

A settlement rail that does not depend on US banking. This is the one that nobody in the listicles mentions. Tricount, Settle Up, Splid all support multi-currency. None of them settle the money. They are trackers. Once the trip ends, you are back to PayPal, Wise, or "we'll figure it out next time we see each other."

## The Splitwise alternative shortlist for international trips

**Tricount** is the obvious pick for pure tracking. Better multi-currency than Splitwise, cleaner interface, owned by bunq (an actual European bank, so the company is not going anywhere). No daily expense limit. No ads. If your group is happy to settle on the side via Wise or cash, this is the lowest-friction option.

**Settle Up** is good when you have unequal splits or unreliable data. The offline mode is best-in-class. Custom percentage splits and itemized splits work without paying.

**Splid** is the no-account option. Create a group, share the link, anyone can log expenses. 150+ currencies. Works fully offline. Best for one-time trips with people you don't normally split money with.

**Cino** uses a shared virtual card that splits payments at the point of purchase. Interesting model. Requires every group member to onboard, which is a significant ask.

The thing none of these solve: settlement. They all stop where Splitwise stops, just with cleaner UX. If your group lives in different countries, every one of these still leaves you with the "now what" problem at the end of the trip.

## What you should actually do

If you're traveling domestically in the US, Splitwise Pro is fine. Pay the $60.

If you're traveling internationally with people who all live in the US, Splitwise Pro plus Wise transfers when you get home works. Accept the rate margin and the time cost.

If your group includes people who live in different countries, Tricount or Settle Up for tracking, then settle via Wise (free for under $9k a year) or Splito (instant, on-chain).

If your group is crypto-native, Splito.

Splitwise was built for a single-currency American group splitting a dinner bill. It is good at that. The further your trip is from that situation, the worse it gets, and no number of listicles is going to change that. The right Splitwise alternative for an international trip is the one that handles the part Splitwise doesn't: getting the money from one person to another, across borders, without you having to figure it out yourself.
